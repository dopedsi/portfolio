Background

It was announced in 2021 that National Rail would be rebranded as Great British Railways and become a state-run company. Growing up in the UK, I had experienced pain points with the company for most of my life and when taking a look at their app and website, that was still very much the case.
What better time to do a complete overhaul.

The challenge

To completely redesign both the app and website without any contraints.

Discovery

I conducted user surveys, interviews and usability tests to discover what users pain points were. The most common frustrations were:

  • the UI design being outdated
  • having to leave the app/website in order to buy a ticket
  • nothing working as they should


    • In the usability tests, abandonment rate was extremely high as users gave up on tasks through sheer frustration.

    I had found through secondary research, that the company was moving from paper tickets to plastic cards, called smart cards, as well as e-tickets and m-tickets. I wanted to make sure I incorporated this feature in my design.

    To ensure that customers retained some familiarity with the booking form, I conducted a competitor analysis and found that the most common version was a left-aligned vertical input form, although horizontal, center-aligned forms were also common.
